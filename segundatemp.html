THE BOYS is an irreverent take on what happens when superheroes, who are as popular as celebrities, as influential as politicians and as revered as gods, abuse their superpowers rather than using them for good. It's powerless versus superpower, as the boys embark on a heroic journey to expose the truth about "The Seven" with the support of Vought.

Atores principais Karl Urban, Jack Quaid, Antony Starr
Gêneros Ficção científica, Comédia, Drama, Ação
Legendas Engles(EUA),Português (Brasil)
Idiomas de áudio Engles(EUA),Português (Brasil)

STAN EDGAR

Stan Edgar began his career as an associate for “Vought American” in 1984. Calm, collected, yet ruthless, he rose to become chief Executive for the modern day Vought International. As pretty much the only person who’s not afraid of Homelander, Edgar is the cool-headed puppet-master with a shadowy past; he pulls the strings that move Vought forward—no matter the cost.

Ashley-Barrett

Ashley’s had a tough job over the past year. She’s still working as the Vice President of Hero Management at Vought, but her primary responsibility is Homelander. And since the news about Stormfront came out a year ago, and Homelander’s approval ratings took a dive because of it, Ashley’s not only had to manage his volatile publicity, she’s also had to tiptoe around his nuclear temper.

STARLIGHT

Despite everything she’s been through, Annie January is still as down-to-earth and sincere as they come; the girl next door with superpowers. Today, she’s one of America’s favorite superheroes, polling at a 96% approval rating, a new Vought record! But the popularity, poll numbers, and brand endorsements mean nothing to her — because all she wants is the same thing she’s always wanted: to save the world.

HOMELANDER

Since the news broke about Stormfront’s Nazi past, Homelander’s been on a year-long apology tour. Now his approval ratings are in the toilet, and worst of all, he’s had to play nice with everyone after Queen Maeve blackmailed him with the Flight 37 video. But the most powerful man in the world is fed up with being restrained – and it’s only a matter of time before his rage boils over.

Black-Noire

Silent, stealthy, and enigmatic. Black Noir is The Seven’s superpowered ninja. His superstrength and heightened senses are matched only by his proficiency in the martial arts. Little is known about this lethal warrior, other than his having trained with a master sensei to become the death dealer that he is today. Whatever lies behind the mask is a complete mystery – which only makes him more terrifying.

QUEEN MAEVE

After using the Flight 37 video to blackmail Homelander, Queen Maeve has helped keep a lid on his psycho behavior and bought everyone some much-needed breathing room. But underneath her tough and cynical exterior, Maeve is getting worried. She can see the cracks forming in Homelander’s self-control, and she knows the Flight 37 video won’t hold him back forever.

A-TRAIN

Even though the Church of the Collective helped get him back into The Seven, despite his secret heart issues, A-Train still has one big problem: the fastest man in the world can’t run anymore. Fans, paparazzi, and reporters are all asking when they’re going to see him race again. And A-Train’s running out of excuses. But can he find a way to stay relevant as a superhero when he no longer has his superpower?

THE DEEP

After defecting from the Church of the Collective, The Deep has become a favorite on the national talk show circuit with the success of his (ghost-written) memoir, Deeper, about his escape from the cult. And with his brilliant wife, Cassandra, coaching him along, he’s finally getting the recognition he thinks he deserves. But none of that compares to being a hero in The Seven. And he wants back in. Bad.

Soldier-Boy

He was America’s first and greatest superhero before Homelander. He helped good triumph over evil in WWII. With his superteam, Payback, he fought for liberty and justice for all, until heroically sacrificing his own life to save America from a nuclear plant meltdown in 1984. But behind the glossy myth, there’s a lot the world doesn’t know about Soldier Boy — and some things are better left in the past.

BILLY BUTCHER

Thanks to the Flight 37 video keeping Homelander under control, every one of The Boys is living their best lives. Except for Butcher. He’s still keeping tabs on supes in his job with the FBSA, but he’s frustrated with all the political compromising. Butcher still wants one thing and one thing only: to destroy all superheroes, especially Homelander – and there’s no line he won’t cross to get what he wants.

MOTHER’S MILK / M.M.

Mother’s Milk has finally put the war on superheroes behind him. Now he works normal 9 to 5, and lives a quiet life where he’s not constantly looking over his shoulder. Even though his wife, Monique, met someone else and moved on, M.M. is back in his daughter's life. But when a ghost from his past comes back to haunt him, M.M. will have to decide if he can continue to look the other way in the war on supes.

FRENCHIE

For the past year, Frenchie’s been working with Kimiko and Butcher at the FBSA, keeping tabs on supes, and living as normal a life as he can. Things are good. Until an unwanted visitor throws his entire life out of balance. He’ll have to figure out who he really is — someone who follows orders, no matter how horrible, or a man who stands up for himself and the people he cares about, consequences be damned.

Kimiko

Even though Kimiko can’t use her voice, she finally has one true friend in the world she can talk to, since she taught Frenchie her unique sign language. But she still misses her little brother, Kenji, and she aches for the life of innocence that was stolen from her when she was turned into a “monster” with Compound V by the Shining Light Liberation Army. But can she find happiness despite that?

HUGH “HUGHIE” CAMPBELL

Hughie got himself a nice apartment, a Peloton, and his relationship with Starlight is better than ever. On top of all that, he’s got a great job at the FBSA, being Butcher’s boss! But most importantly, he’s making a difference — collateral damage caused by supes is down by an astonishing 60%. Life is good. And he did it all above board, not covered in blood and guts like he used to be with The Boys.

VICTORIA “VIC” NEUMAN

As the Director of the Federal Bureau of Superhuman Affairs, Congresswoman Neuman helps make the world a safer place. By compromising with Vought, she’s been able to root out the bad apples amongst the supes, which has meant more people alive and at home with their families at the end of every day. Now, with her soaring political ambitions — and popularity — there’s no limit to what she can achieve.
